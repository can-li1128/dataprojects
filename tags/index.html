Project 4: Brexit Poll Data Analysis

Overview In June 2016, the United Kingdom (UK) held a referendum to determine whether the country would “Remain” in or “Leave” the European Union (EU). This referendum is commonly known as Brexit. Although the media and others interpreted poll results as forecasting “Remain” ( p>0.5, p indicating the proportion voted “Remain”) , the actual proportion that voted “Remain” was only 48.1% (p=0.481) and the UK thus voted to leave the EU.

Project 2: Systolic Blood Pressure

Overview This report analyzes blood pressure data from the survey collected by the United States National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS). This center has conducted a series of health and nutrition surveys since the 1960’s. Starting in 1999, about 5,000 individuals of all ages have been interviewed every year and then they complete the health examination component of the survey. Part of this dataset is made available via the NHANES package.

Project 1: Iris Identification

Overview Applying machine learning method, this project takes sepal and petal measurement data from the famous (Fisher’s or Anderson’s) iris data set to predict species, versicolor or virginica. A continuous sequence of cutoff points were used to find the best measurement that produces highest accuracy on training data; then the same measurement was applied to test data to evaluate the algorithm. Two specific features are proved to be most useful in determining iris species.
