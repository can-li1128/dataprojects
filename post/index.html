Projects

I started learning data science on the August long weekend of 2020 as my pandemic project. As soon, I found myself immersed in various projects, excited about what can be discovered and understood.

In documenting these selected projects, I demonstrate various data science methodologies used in these projects, from simple computation to statistical methods to machine learning.

Project background is described in overview; although not every step is detailed, R packages, data set, and important code chucks used in each projects are listed. These documents are produced in R Markdown reports.

Project 5: Titanic Decision Tree

The Titanic was a British ocean liner that struck an iceberg and sunk on its maiden voyage in 1912 from the United Kingdom to New York. More than 1,500 of the estimated 2,224 passengers and crew died in the accident, making this one of the largest maritime disasters ever outside of war. The ship carried a wide range of passengers of all ages and both genders, from luxury travelers in first-class to immigrants in the lower classes.

Project 4: Brexit Poll Data Analysis

In June 2016, the United Kingdom (UK) held a referendum to determine whether the country would “Remain” in or “Leave” the European Union (EU). This referendum is commonly known as Brexit. Although the media and others interpreted poll results as forecasting “Remain” ( p>0.5, p indicating the proportion voted “Remain”) , the actual proportion that voted “Remain” was only 48.1% (p=0.481) and the UK thus voted to leave the EU.
